The District Attorney's office arraigned and charged 46 individuals with criminal trespassing for occupying Hamilton Hall. Spectator analyzed the affiliation of the arrested individuals.

Though the District Attorney’s office provided 46 docket numbers for those arrested and charged inside of Hamilton Hall, one of the docket numbers did not produce an active case when searched.

Thirty-three are current students or staff at Columbia.

The University first identified 14 undergraduates.

However, after an inquiry from Spectator regarding the discrepancy between the number of undergraduates, a University spokesperson released updated figures, identifying 15.

The University excluded Barnard students from their official undergraduate count, identifying them instead as members of a Columbia-affiliated institution. Spectator’s analysis includes Barnard students in its count of 18 undergraduate students at Columbia College, The School of Engineering and Applied Sciences, General Studies, or Barnard College.

Eight are graduate students.

Five are students at Union Theological Seminary, an affiliated institution.

Two are staff employed by the University.

Ten are either recent alumni of Columbia or another nearby New York City University.

Five are recent alumnus of Columbia, graduating within the past 4 years. The University and the New York Police Department identified these individuals as “unaffiliated.”

Five are current student or recent alumni of other NYC universities, including New York University and the City College of New York.

Two have no immediately clear connection to the University or other New York City colleges, but have a documented history of participating in pro-Palestinian protests, according to Spectator’s analysis.
